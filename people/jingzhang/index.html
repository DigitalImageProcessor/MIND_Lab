Scientific Research Work：

  1. Since September 2013, as Principal Investigator (PI), I have led the NSFC General Program “Mechanism of Beam Hardening Effects on CBCT-based Alveolar Bone Strength Assessment”; served as Sub-project PI for the National High-Tech R&D Program (863 Program) “Mixed Reality-Based Training and Real-Time Decision System for Minimally Invasive Brain/Spinal Cord Surgery”; obtained two Sichuan Provincial Sci-Tech Projects (“Key Technologies for Laparoscopic Surgery Simulation” and “Key Technologies for Craniomaxillofacial Surgery Simulation”); and directed multiple additional grant-funded and industry-sponsored research initiatives.

  2. From September 2009 to August 2012, as Sub-project Lead for “Image Processing & Modeling” under Singapore’s National Research Foundation project “Computer- and Robot-Assisted Surgical Simulation System” (total funding: SGD 3 million), I managed proposal development, budgeting, and competitive bidding, delivering world-leading outcomes in laparoscopic surgery simulation and securing two international patents.

  3. From May to September 2009, as a Senior Research Fellow for the Singapore Ministry of Health project “Osteoporosis Study in Chinese and Indian Populations,” I completed CT image segmentation, calibration, and bone mineral density calculations for 120 patients. I innovatively proposed and implemented a monoenergetic beam hardening correction algorithm to calibrate medical CT images, achieving results matching dual-energy X-ray absorptiometry (DXA) accuracy. The first-author paper from this study was accepted by the top-tier journal IEEE Transactions on Medical Imaging .
